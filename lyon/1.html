RESUME: Mission - Lyon, France

  1. The City of Lyon
    1. Founded in 43 B.C. by order of Julius Caesar
    2. Today 2nd-largest city in France: pop. 1.6 million (inc. suburbs)
    3. A crossroads for all Europe
  2. Church History in Lyon
    1. Ancient history: Irenaeus : an elder in Lyon in the 2nd century
    2. Modern history
      May 1975 - Arlin & Judy Hendrix and Max Dauner began the work in Lyon.
      April 1977 - Max married the former Prisca Daugherty.
      August 1982 - Mike & Karen Mason came to help the Lyon mission. In 1986, they moved to Nantes, France.
      July 1984 - Max & Monique Lanoix, converted in Lyon in 1981, moved to Guadeloupe (French Caribbean) and started a congregation.
      September 1984 - Barry & Rachel Baggott arrived to work for 2 years with the Hendrixes. (They then worked as missionaries in Ivory Coast for 15 years.)
      September 1986 - Scott Harris & Jeff Wilson arrived to work 2 years with the church. (Scott & his wife Lisa later worked as missionaries in Togo, West Africa, and they now work in South Africa.)
      October 1987 - Charles & Pam White moved from Grenoble to Lyon.
      January 1988 - Judy Hendrix died of cancer.
      December 1988 - Arlin Hendrix married the former Pamela Whitesell.
      April 1989 - Max & Prisca Dauner moved to help the mission in Montpellier, France.
      August 2001 - Archie & Mandy Chankin and Andy & Melissa Johnson arrived for 1 year of language study. (They are presently missionaries in Burkina Faso, West Africa.)
      January 2002 - Aaron & Andrea Burk arrived for 4 months of language study. (They are presently missionaries in Burkina Faso, West Africa.)
      August 2003 - Chad & Amy Carter arrived for 6 months of language study. (They are presently missionaries in Burkina Faso, West Africa.)
      September 2004 - Katie Kelly arrived for 2 years as a missions apprentice.
 
"God willing, ours is a long-term, perhaps lifetime commitment to the work in France and the French-speaking world." -- Arlin & Pamela Hendrix
